Overview

Neo-Tokyo, the sprawling megacity that rose from the ashes of Old Tokyo after the Great Quake of 2051. A neon-lit metropolis where cutting-edge technology and ancient traditions collide, Neo-Tokyo is a city of stark contrasts - towering skyscrapers and cramped alleyways, opulent luxuries and grinding poverty, dazzling innovations and dark conspiracies.

Geography and Architecture

Spanning the entire Kanto region, Neo-Tokyo is a vast urban expanse:

  • The Spires: Gleaming skyscrapers housing corporate headquarters and luxury apartments.
  • The Sprawl: Dense, multi-level urban areas where most of the population resides.
  • The Undercity: Subterranean levels built in the aftermath of the Great Quake.
  • Neo-Tokyo Bay: Massive seawalls protect the city from rising sea levels and house extensive arcologies.

Society and Culture

Neo-Tokyo's society is a complex tapestry of old and new:

  • Augmentation Culture: Cybernetic enhancements are commonplace, blurring the line between human and machine.
  • Neo-Shintoism: A modern revival of traditional beliefs, adapted to the cyberpunk era.
  • Virtual Communities: Many citizens spend significant time in VR environments, some permanently "jacked in".
  • Class Divide: Extreme wealth inequality creates tension between corporate elites and the struggling masses.

Technology and Infrastructure

Neo-Tokyo is at the forefront of technological innovation:

  • The Grid: An advanced neural network connecting all of Neo-Tokyo's systems and many of its citizens.
  • Maglev Trains: High-speed magnetic levitation trains form the backbone of public transportation.
  • Holographic Displays: Pervasive AR and holographic technology for advertising and information.
  • AI Governance: Many city functions are managed by artificial intelligences.

Economy and Corporations

The city's economy is dominated by massive corporations:

  • Arasaka Corporation: Security and defense conglomerate with significant political influence.
  • Kaneda Industries: Leaders in cybernetic augmentation and biotechnology.
  • Neuromancer Systems: Pioneers in artificial intelligence and virtual reality technology.
  • Shimada Robotics: Major manufacturer of industrial and personal robotics.

Underworld and Crime

Beneath the glittering surface, Neo-Tokyo has a thriving criminal underworld:

  • Yakuza Clans: Traditional crime syndicates adapted to the digital age.
  • Net Runners: Elite hackers who navigate and exploit The Grid.
  • Black Clinics: Underground medical facilities specializing in illegal cybernetic enhancements.
  • Data Smugglers: Traffickers of sensitive information and restricted technologies.
